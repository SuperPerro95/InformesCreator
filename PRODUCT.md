# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Argentine primary and secondary school teachers working under tight deadlines at the end of each trimester or semester. They sit at school desks or kitchen tables, often late in the evening, translating weeks of classroom observations into formal progress reports for each student. Many are not tech-savvy. They use Windows laptops or desktops, often shared family machines. Their emotional state when using this tool is stressed, time-pressured, and seeking relief from repetitive writing work.

## Product Purpose

InformesCreator uses local AI (Ollama) to turn raw classroom observations and structured questionnaires into polished student progress reports. It exists because writing 30 individual reports by hand is draining and error-prone. Success looks like a teacher finishing an entire class's reports in one sitting, feeling that each report sounds like their own voice, not a robot's.

## Brand Personality

Warm, supportive, efficient

A teaching companion, not a cold tool. The interface should feel like a well-organized colleague who's done this before: calm under pressure, clear about next steps, never making you guess. Efficiency doesn't mean sterile. Warmth doesn't mean whimsical. The personality is a quiet confidence that says "I've got this part, you focus on your students."

## Anti-references

Not Salesforce, not SAP, not any gray-on-gray enterprise dashboard with 12 nested menus and zero personality. No cold corporate SaaS that makes teachers feel like they're filing tax returns instead of writing about their students.

## Design Principles

1. **Quiet competence over spectacle.** Animations and feedback exist to confirm actions, not to entertain. A checkmark beats a confetti cannon.
2. **Progressive disclosure, always.** Teachers should never face a wall of 30 options. Show what's needed now, reveal the rest on demand.
3. **Respect the task rhythm.** Report-writing is repetitive and focus-dependent. Don't interrupt flow for delight. Delight lives in transitions and completions, not in the middle of a form.
4. **Friendly without being childish.** Warm colors and approachable language, but professional enough that a school principal could see the screen over a teacher's shoulder and take the tool seriously.
5. **阿根廷 context, always.** Spanish-language UI, Argentine school terminology, cultural sensitivity for the educational system. Never translate idioms; write them natively.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Sufficient contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support. Respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Teachers may be working on older hardware with smaller screens, so responsive behavior matters. Color is never the sole indicator of state; icons and text labels always accompany status changes.
