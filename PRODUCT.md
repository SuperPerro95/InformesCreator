# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Teachers (docentes) in Argentina, Spanish-speaking primary and secondary school settings. Their context is classroom management — juggling lesson planning, student assessment, and administrative reporting. The primary job to be done is writing individual student reports efficiently, reducing hours of paperwork to minutes. The emotional goal is calm: removing the stress of repetitive report writing so they can focus on teaching.

## Product Purpose

InformesCreator is an AI-assisted tool that helps teachers write personalized student reports. Teachers manage courses, track student observations (attendance, behavior, participation), complete pedagogical questionnaires, and generate detailed natural-language reports using local AI models. Success means a teacher finishes their reporting workload in a fraction of the time, with reports that sound human and personal.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, serene. Three words: **steady, warm, competent**. The tone is respectful and supportive — never condescending. This is a tool that trusts the teacher's judgment and amplifies their work, not a flashy AI novelty. The interface should feel like a well-organized desk, not a control panel.

## Anti-references

- **Cold enterprise software.** SAP, bank portals, HR dashboards — the gray, dense, impersonal aesthetic of corporate tools. No heavy table borders, no cramped data grids, no "admin panel" feel.
- **Toy-like edtech.** Cartoonish illustrations, gamification badges, childish colors. Teachers are professionals; the tool should treat them as such.
- **Generic SaaS.** The Bootstrap-template look, the cookie-cutter sidebar-and-topbar layout with no soul.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm before flashy.** Every screen should reduce cognitive load, not add to it. Generous whitespace, restrained color, clear hierarchy. The tool works quietly in the background.

2. **Trust the teacher.** The teacher knows their students. InformesCreator assists, never dictates. No obtrusive prompts, no "optimization" suggestions that interrupt flow. Controls are available but not demanding attention.

3. **Paper as a metaphor, not a constraint.** Reports are the output, so the reading experience should feel familiar (think clean document, readable type). But the tool itself should not imitate a paper form — it's a digital assistant that happens to produce documents.

4. **Spanish-first, not Spanish-translated.** The entire experience is in Argentine Spanish, with natural phrasing that matches how teachers actually talk about their work. No anglicisms, no awkward translations.

5. **Resilient by default.** Teachers use this in varied environments — offline, on older hardware, with spotty connectivity. The interface never leaves them stranded. Loading states, error recovery, and progressive enhancement are not afterthoughts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 text, 3:1 large text), keyboard-navigable forms and wizards, clear focus indicators, screen-reader-friendly labels and ARIA landmarks. The primary user base (teachers) may include users with visual impairments or motor difficulties, so form interactions and the multi-step wizard must be operable without relying on pointer precision or color alone.
